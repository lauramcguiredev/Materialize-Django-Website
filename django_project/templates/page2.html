Installation Guide

Django Installation

Step 1: Create Account with Digital Ocean

The first step to deploying your website is to sign up with a web host. For this tutorial we will be using Digital Ocean as our host provider. Go to https://digitalocean.com and sign up using one of the suggested methods on their homepage.

Step 2: Navigate to Create Droplet

Next is to deploy a droplet, this is what Digital Ocean calls their virtual machines. Click on the green "Create" tab and select "Droplets" from the drop down menu.

Step 3: Select Django Droplet

On the "Create Droplets" page under the "Choose an image" heading click on "Marketplace". Then click on "See all Marketplace Apps". Here you search for the "Django" framework. Click on the "Django" framework and finally select "Create Django Droplet". From here you are taken back to the "Create Droplets" page.

Step 4: Configure and Create Droplet

Go through the rest of the instructions and select the options which suit your need. Once you have finished configuring your droplet scroll to the bottom of the page and click "Create Droplet". Just like that you have create a fully installed, configured, and ready to go Django framework.

Materialize Setup

Step 1: Copy the CDN

Navigate to https://materializecss.com/getting-started.html and scroll down to the section named CDN. Put the compiled and minified CSS in the header of your HTML files. Put the compiled and minified Javascript at the end of the body tag to optimize loading times. Including the latest CDN allows you full access to all of the features Materialize offers.


This is the CSS part that you put in the head part of your HTML files:

<link rel="stylesheet" href="https://cdnjs.cloudflare.com/ajax/libs/materialize/1.0.0/css/materialize.min.css">

This is the JavaScript part you put before the end of your body tag:

<script src="https://cdnjs.cloudflare.com/ajax/libs/materialize/1.0.0/js/materialize.min.js"></script>
